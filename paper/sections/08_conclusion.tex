\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented GAME-Mal, a malware family classifier that combines
reflection-aware dynamic-trace parsing with a sigmoid-gated
multi-head attention transformer. The gated architecture — G1
position of Qiu et al.\ — not only matches a strong Random Forest
baseline on the eight-family UMD subset but also exposes an
intrinsic, forward-pass-native explanation of its decisions
through its gate activations. On the minority ransomware family
(Fusob) in particular, GAME-Mal improves macro F1 by over 0.4
absolute points compared to a faithful reproduction of the
Markov associative-rule classifier of D'Angelo et al., while
producing behaviourally coherent per-family API fingerprints
without any post-hoc attribution.

The broader contribution is methodological: gated attention is a
mechanism that \emph{trades a small amount of representational
capacity for a meaningful amount of interpretability}. On
security-critical applications where model behaviour must be
explainable to a human analyst, this trade is often the one
worth making.
