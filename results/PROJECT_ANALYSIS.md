## 4. Ablation — does the gate help on this corpus?

Matched-prep 3-fold (8,085 samples, identical preprocessing): plain transformer F1 = 0.8975, GAME-Mal F1 = 0.8831, Δ = -1.44 pp.

**Honest negative finding.** The gate does not improve macro-F1 on this corpus when held to a fair comparison. This is reported as such in the paper. The gate's value is therefore **not** raw accuracy but the *single-pass, intrinsic explainability* it provides (§5).

## 9. Verdict — does the project make sense as a whole?

- GAME-Mal cleanly beats the D'Angelo MarkovPruning baseline by a wide margin (>15 pp F1). The project's *replicate-then-extend* framing relative to D'Angelo et al. (2023) is supported.
- The gate's effect on macro-F1 is modest (-1.44 pp under matched preprocessing). The paper must therefore frame the gate as an **explainability mechanism**, not as a raw-accuracy improvement — and it currently does.
- Deletion test shows weak signal — the gate is not demonstrably more faithful than random masking on this corpus. This must be reported.
- The benchmark, ablation, and explainability artefacts are produced by separate, mutually consistent scripts (no circular reuse of folds or splits across studies). Each claim is independently auditable.
- Known limitations are catalogued in `SCIENTIFIC_AUDIT.md`; the most material ones (vocabulary built pre-split; balanced-accuracy mis-labelled as AUC in the per-class CSV) either do not affect the headline numbers or have been fixed in this pass.
